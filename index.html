Billie Eilish Pirate Baird O’Connell was born on December 18, 2001, in Los Angeles, California, where she also grew up. She comes from a creative family: her mother Maggie Baird is an actress and teacher, her father Patrick O’Connell is an actor and musician, and her older brother Finneas O’Connell is a singer, songwriter, and producer who works closely with her on almost all of her music. Billie was homeschooled, which gave her the freedom to focus on music, songwriting, and artistic development from a very young age.

Passion for dance

From childhood, Billie was deeply interested in dance and trained in hip-hop and contemporary styles. Dance was an important part of her life until she suffered a serious hip injury at the age of thirteen. Because of this injury, she had to stop dancing, which was emotionally difficult for her, but it pushed her to concentrate more fully on music as her main creative outlet. Also, she still dances in her clips and just for fun.

Music and awards

Billie’s music career began to gain attention in 2015 when she uploaded the song “Ocean Eyes,” written and produced by her brother Finneas, to SoundCloud. The song quickly went viral and launched her career. She released her debut EP Don’t Smile at Me in 2017, followed by her highly successful albums When We All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go? in 2019, Happier Than Ever in 2021, and Hit Me Hard and Soft in 2024.

Billie has received numerous awards, including multiple Grammy Awards, and in 2020 she became the youngest artist in history to win the four main Grammy categories in a single year. Also she won Record of the Year in 2021 for 'Everything I Wanted' and later earned a Grammy for 'No Time to Die', the James Bond theme song. In 2026 she won her Grammy Nomination of Song of the year with her 'WILDFLOWER'. By a very young age, she had already won several Grammy Awards, confirming that her success and influence in music have been long-lasting.

Tourette syndrome

Billie Eilish has Tourette syndrome, a neurological condition that causes involuntary motor and vocal tics. She was diagnosed as a child and later decided to speak publicly about it in order to reduce misunderstanding and stigma. Over time, she learned to accept her condition as part of who she is.

Support

In addition to her music, Billie is known for her activism. She is vegan and strongly supports animal rights, environmental protection, and climate change awareness. She has donated money and used her platform to support mental health initiatives, social justice movements, and ecological causes.

At the WSJ’s Music Innovator award ceremony, she said “If you’re a billionaire, why are you a billionaire? No hate, but yeah# Give your money away, shorties”, and donated 11.5 million on charity.

Tattoos

Billie also has several tattoos, each with personal meaning, including designs that reflect her individuality, self-expression, and body autonomy.
